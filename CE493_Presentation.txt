Root–Leaf Clock Spatial Delay Variation
During a VDD droop, the root clock experiences the delay
change first, while the leaf sees a shifted version of the
disturbance.
Leaf clock has additional distribution delay, it naturally
introduces clock–data compensation across several cycles.
DVM_ROOT detects timing failures earlier than DVM_LEAF

Lesson from Backend Implementation
Power Integrity in Custom Layout
LVS passed, but power routing was
too weak: narrow VDD/VSS lines
and no mesh structure.
Severe IR drop caused large delay
variation across all nets in the
design.
How we solve the problem?
Widening the M1/M2 rails,
Adding Metal-3 VDD&VSS straps
Inserting additional vias.

Conclusion
Implemented an all-digital adaptive clocking system from RTL to post-layout
simulation.
Tunable-length delay provided controllable clock–data compensation under droop.
DVM with dual TRCs successfully detected VDD droops at root and leaf nodes.
Backend work revealed power-integrity issues (IR drop), fixed by strengthening
VDD/VSS routing.
